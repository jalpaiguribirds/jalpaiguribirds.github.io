North Sikkim

Oxbow Lake Formation

The Place


A major part of the North Sikkim landscape falls under the Kanchenjunga Biosphere Reserve. This is the region where the Himalayas give way to the Tibetan Plateaumakinga diverse landscape within a small area. The Lachung area is characterised by wide valleys, the most prominent being the Yumthang valley which is reached by passing through the Singba Rhododendron Sanctuary. The Sanctuary boasts of at least 30 species of Rhododendrons and is also home to a variety of birds like Tits, Warblers, Sunbirds& Pheasants.

Another road diverges from Chungthang towards the village of Lachen and proceeds further to the Gurudrongmar Lake in the Tibetan Plateau. The landscape dramatically changes beyond Thangu. From here begins the trans-Himalayan Zone. The region is home to a variety of Birds & Mammals typical of the Cold Desert.


Bird Life


North Sikkim is one of the best birding destinations for someone looking for high altitude species in the Eastern Himalayas. As the road from Gangtok to Chungthang generally remains a bit heavy with traffic, serious birding can start only after Chungthang.

Ground-Pecker


Morning birding around the village of Lachung will yield species like Black-faced Laughinhthrush, White Collared Blackbird, Dark-breasted Rosefinch, Grey-backed Shrike, Spotted Nutcracker,Yellow-billed Blue Magpie etc. Further towards Yumthang in the Rhodo forest, birds like Whistler’s Warber,Hume’s Warbler, Rufous-vented Tit, Coal Tit, Fire-tailed Sunbird, Rufous-gorgetedFlycatcher, Red-headed Bullfinch etc. are not uncommon. The Yumthang valley is worth looking for breeding Ibisbill& Broad-billed Warblers. Red-billed Choughs are more common here than their Yellow-billed cousins. With luck on your side you may see your first Grandala which sometimes flock in numbers more than 50.

Rufous-necked Snowfinch

On the otherside of the ridge, the Lachen Valley is more forested. The Bamboo brakes just at the entrance of the village is a sure shot area for Brown Parrotbills. Lachen is also one of the best sites to see the Speckled Wood Pigeon apart from the other commoners found in the Lachung Valley. Flocks of Scarlet Finch have also been observed here during July & August. The habitat between Lachen&Thangu has got a bit disturbed by the ongoing road construction works. Previously Blood Pheasant, Red Crossbill, Snow Pigeonetc. have been observed along this stretch from time to time. Along the road to Gurudrongmar from Thangu lookout for species like Ibisbill, Hume’s Groundpecker, Streaked Rosefinch, Snow Pigeon, Red-billed Chough, Horned Lark etc. The rare White-rumped Snowfinch is best looked for around the Pika burrows. The area around Gurudrongmar is particularly good for White-throated Dipper ( both races), Hume Short-toed Lark, Horned Lark, Brandt’s Mountain Finch, Plain Mountain Finch, the rare Rufous-necked Snowfinch, Robin Accentor etc. This is also good site for the White-winged Redstart. Ruddy-shelduck has been observed to breed here.

The Mammal life here is also very interesting. Keep your eyes open for species like the Tibetan Wild Ass, Mountain Weasel, Argali, Tibetan Gazelle, Red Fox, Pallas Cat, Himalayan Marmot, Royle’sPika and Black-lipped Pika.

Vegetation


The relatively lower altitudes of North Sikkim is covered with Sub-tropical Semi-Evergreen and Broadleaved Hill Forests. With the increase in altitude this gives way to sub-Alpine and Alpine vegetation.
The stretch between LachungandYumthang has one of the best Rhododendron covers in the area. Beyond Thangu, the treeline ceases and is replaced by Alpine shrubs consisting of Primulas, Poppy etc.
The Cold Desert area is devoid of vegetation apart from the Lichen that grows on the rock faces in a variety of colours. During July-August the plateau flourishes with a variety of grasses attracting a broad spectrum of Birds & Mammals.

Get In


There are no airports or railway stations in Sikkim. The only way to enter Sikkim from rest of India is by road using bus or jeep from West Bengal.
Nearest airport for Sikkim is Bagdogra, near Siliguri in North Bengal, which is 124 km and approximately four hours drive from Gangtok.
Meanwhile, the nearest railway stations are Siliguri and New Jalpaiguri. Both of these have a number of trains connecting them to Kolkata. You should be able to get pre-paid taxis to Gangtok outside the railway stations easily. The Sikkim government has fixed rate (and fixed schedule) buses plying between Siliguri and Gangtok.


Best Time To Visit


The most favourable birding season is from May to early October though some recent winter visits have been quite productive too.


Keep In Mind


Since the major part of North Sikkim shares its boundary with China, this is a restricted zone, and one will require special permit for visiting this area. Foreigners are allowed uptoYumthang beyond Lachung and up toThangu and Chopta Valley beyond Lachen. In general, North Sikkim is visited by package tours conducted by the local tour operators operating from Gantok. For birdwatching, talk to your operator in advance and plan the duration of your stay accordingly. Two nights at Lachen and two nights at Lachung is a minimal time that will be required for a birding tour. Gurugrongmar Lake is situated at an altitude of 17800 feet and one might experience dizziness, nausea etc. which are initial symptoms of High Altitude sickness. It is advised to get down to lower altitudes on signs of such symptoms. There are many Photography Prohibited Zones along this route; it is therefore advisable to ask for permission while birding around Military Camp areas.
