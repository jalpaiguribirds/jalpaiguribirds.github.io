Buxa Tiger Reserve

Buxa Hills

The Place


Buxa tiger Reserve is the largest protected area of Northern West Bengal. It is in the far North-Eastern corner of the state. The Reserve extends from the Pana River in the West to Sankosh River and Assam in the East. This area is bound by Bhutan in the North, whose hills extend well into reserve. The majority of the reserve is in the plains surrounded by tea gardens and villages. The hill forests of Buxa Tiger Reserve reach an approx. altitude of 2000 m near its border with Bhutan and continues further probably up to Tale La at 4000 mts. This area is also the extended part of the Greater Manas landscape and both are still connected through the Phipsoo Wildlife Sanctuary of Bhutan.

All these parameters combine to make Buxa Tiger Reserve a land of great floral and faunal diversity.The variation in altitude and vegetation attract a lot variety of birds like Hornbill, Pitta, Cochoa, Broadbill, Parrotbill etc. to make this area a true hotspot for any bird watcher.


Bird Life


Contiguous forests from the plains to nearly 2000 mts at the Bhutanese border, make Buxa Tiger Reserve a place of unique avian diversity. The road that enters the reserve at Rajabhatkhawa and continues to Jainty is particularly good for Wreathed and Great Pied Hornbill, Blue-bearded Bee-eater, Collared Falconet, Crested Goshawk, Pin-tailed Green Pigeon, White-rumpedShama, Scarlet Minivet, Abott’s Babbler etc.

Sultan Tit


From Jainty along the river bed towards Mahakal you may find specialities like Crested Kingfisher, Long-billed Plover (W), Wallcreper (W), Rufous-bellied Eagle etc. The patch of forest between Jainty and Bhutia Busty is good for lower hill species like Green Magpie, Grey-throated Babbler, White-browed Scimitar Babbler, Dollar Bird etc. Crested Serpent Eagle is the most common raptor. Although not very common, summer visitors like Oriental Dwarf Kingfisher, Ruddy Kingfisher and Malayan Night Heron has also been recorded here. The commoners here include the Asian Fairy Blue bird, Blue-throated, Lineated& Coppersmith Barbets, Greater & Lesser Racket-tailed Drongo, Spangle Drongo, Large Cuckoo Shrike, Black Hooded Oriole etc.

Himalayan Cutia

But the real haven of Bird diversity of BTR lies in its hills. From Santarabari it’s a 4⅟2 kms hike to Buxaduar, the site of the historic Buxa Fort. It is a small village with an old post office and a FRH. Birds around this place include Great Pied Hornbill, Sultan tit, Maroon Oriole, Crimson Sunbird, StreakedSpiderhunter, Small Niltava (W), Little Pied Flycatcher, Green Magpie, Large Woodshrike, Lesser & Greater Yellownape, RuficentPrinia, White-crested Laughingthrush, Pale Blue Flycatcher(W), Chestnut-headed Tesia (W), White-gorgeted Flycatcher (W), Snowy-browed Flycatcher (W), Tickell’s thrush, Scarlet Minivet, Long-tailed Minivet, Grey-chinnedMinivet, Whiskered Yuhina, Red-tailed Minla etc.

For those who are willing to take the strain of trekking to Rupam Valley or Omchupani near Bhutanese border may bag prize catches like Red-headed Trogon, Greater Rufous-headedParrotbill, Green Cochoa, Coral-billed Scimitar Babbler, Rusty-cheeked Scimitar Babbler, Yellow-throated Fulvetta, Asian Emerald Cuckoo, Drongo Cuckoo, Brown-wood Owl, White-napedYuhina etc. This area is also one of the best places to see the Himalayan Cutia and Rufous-backed Sibia apart from other specialities that include Scarlet Finch, Mountain Tailorbird, Golden Babbler, Grey-cheecked Warbler, White-spectacled Warbler, large Niltava, Kaleej Pheasant etc. This area is also good for at least three varieties of Hill Partridges including the Chestnut-breasted Hill Partridge. One has a fair chance to see Rufous-necked Hornbill while on route.

Vegetation


Sal forest dominates the major share of the plains in Buxa Tiger Reserve. There are some patches of Teak plantation but the overall variety here is mainly of Moist-deciduous type with some evergreen patches in between.
This type continues quite some way up in the hills after which it is replaced by wet semi-evergreen type. Beyond Rover’s point at 1500 mts the forest is predominantly of Broad-leaved type all way up to the Bhutanese border.

Get In


Nearest Airport: Bagdogra – 195 km away.
Nearest Rail Station: Alipurduar Junction / New Alipurduar – 37 km.
Nearest Town/ City: Alipurduar – 35 km.
By Road: NH 31 is the main highway 25 km away. One can hire a car from Alipurduar or Rajabhatkhawa, 20 km away.


Best Time To Visit


The weather is sultry throughout the year. Summers are hot with the temperatures reaching a peak in April. There is heavy rainfall from June to August. Pre-monsoon showers in May are an almost unfailing annual feature. The average annual rainfall is about 5,323 mm., much higher than the mean of 3,925 mm. for the rest of the district.
October to May is a good time to visit with March-April probably being the best time, since animals emerge to feed on the new grasses.

Keep In Mind


For a serious bird watcher, the duration of stay at Buxa may vary, but in general 5 to 6 days of birding is good enough for most. For bird watching in the forest of the plains, the stay should be scheduled at Rajabhatkhawa or Jainty, the latter is richer with more variety. However, many of the specialities of Buxa are found mostly in the hills, and one has to take the strain of hill hiking to see them. Camping in the hill forests will reap the richest dividend but of course in exchange of some blood and sweat. Buxaduar acts as the best base for such hill venture and some of the local lodges arrange for such trips will all logistics.
